Option 1: Civil War Museum Exhibit

Create a detailed, museum-style exhibit that explains a Civil War theme, person, or event. Focus on historical accuracy, visual clarity, and storytelling. Include artifacts, images, labels, and a written summary for viewers to understand your exhibit’s topic.

Suggested Materials

  • Poster board or tri-fold display
  • Printed or hand-drawn images
  • Index cards for artifact labels
  • Glue, tape, scissors, markers
  • Access to books or online sources

5-Day Timeline & Checklist

DayTasks
Day 1 Choose topic and begin research.
Day 2 Select 5 key artifacts/images; draft label cards.
Day 3 Build display; add visuals, titles, and labels.
Day 4 Write 1-page curator summary; practice presentation.
Day 5 Present exhibit and submit reflection.

Rubric (40 pts)

  • Historical Accuracy & Research: 15 pts
  • Visual Design & Creativity: 10 pts
  • Clarity of Labels & Summary: 10 pts
  • Presentation & Reflection: 5 pts

Option 2: Civil War Podcast/Interview

Produce a 3–5 minute historically accurate podcast or interview. Students research a topic or character from the Civil War, write a detailed script, and record their presentation. Optional sound effects or background music may enhance the storytelling experience.

Suggested Materials

  • Laptop, tablet, or phone with microphone
  • Recording app (Audacity, GarageBand, Voice Memos)
  • Script template or index cards
  • Background music or sound effects (optional)

5-Day Timeline & Checklist

DayTasks
Day 1 Choose topic & role; research thoroughly.
Day 2 Write complete script including 3+ historical facts.
Day 3 Record audio; add effects if desired.
Day 4 Edit recording; refine script.
Day 5 Share podcast and submit reflection.

Rubric (40 pts)

  • Historical Accuracy & Research: 15 pts
  • Script Quality & Storytelling: 10 pts
  • Recording Clarity & Presentation: 10 pts
  • Reflection: 5 pts

Option 3: Civil War Soldier’s Diary

Create a historically accurate diary from the perspective of a soldier, nurse, or freed person. Include personal experiences, emotions, and events during the Civil War. Enhance your diary with illustrations, maps, or sketches if desired.

Suggested Materials

  • Notebook or digital document
  • Historical maps, timelines, or letters
  • Colored pencils or pens for illustrations
  • Optional: small journal booklet for final copy

5-Day Timeline & Checklist

DayTasks
Day 1 Choose character & research background.
Day 2 Draft first two diary entries with real events.
Day 3 Complete remaining entries including emotions & struggles.
Day 4 Add maps/illustrations and revise entries.
Day 5 Compile final diary & submit reflection.

Rubric (40 pts)

  • Historical Accuracy & Research: 15 pts
  • Creativity & Storytelling: 10 pts
  • Illustrations & Visuals: 10 pts
  • Reflection: 5 pts

Option 4: Civil War Monument/ Memorial

Design a monument or memorial honoring a person, group, or event from the Civil War. Include symbolic elements that explain the significance and meaning. Students should focus on creativity, visual appeal, and historical accuracy.

Suggested Materials

  • Poster board, cardboard, or modeling clay
  • Sketching paper or digital design tool
  • Markers, rulers, paints, glue
  • Reference images of real monuments

5-Day Timeline & Checklist

DayTasks
Day 1 Choose focus & research significance.
Day 2 Draft designs; note symbolism of each element.
Day 3 Build or draw final monument with labels.
Day 4 Write plaque inscription or dedication.
Day 5 Present monument and submit reflection.

Rubric (40 pts)

  • Historical Accuracy & Research: 15 pts
  • Visual Design & Creativity: 10 pts
  • Clarity of Symbolism & Explanation: 10 pts
  • Reflection: 5 pts

Option 5: Civil War News Project

Create a historically accurate and visually engaging Civil War newspaper front page or news broadcast. Include headlines, images, political cartoons, and multiple perspectives from the time. Focus on accuracy, layout, and clarity of information.

Suggested Materials

  • Poster board or Google Slides/Docs
  • Rulers, markers, colored pencils
  • Access to online archives for political cartoons or primary sources
  • Optional: microphone and camera if creating a broadcast

5-Day Timeline & Checklist

DayTasks
Day 1 Choose focus & research key facts/events.
Day 2 Draft 3 news stories including quotes & perspectives.
Day 3 Arrange layout with headlines, columns, images, and cartoon.
Day 4 Add bylines, datelines, color; edit for accuracy.
Day 5 Present front page/broadcast & submit reflection.

Rubric (40 pts)

  • Historical Accuracy & Research: 15 pts
  • Creativity & Design: 10 pts
  • Clarity & Accuracy of Content: 10 pts
  • Reflection: 5 pts
