Option 1: Civil War Museum Exhibit

Create a realistic museum-style display that teaches others about one theme or aspect of the Civil War.

Suggested Materials

  • Poster board or tri-fold display
  • Printed or hand-drawn images
  • Index cards for artifact labels
  • Glue, tape, scissors, markers
  • Access to books or internet sources

5-Day Timeline

DayFocus & Tasks
Day 1Pick exhibit topic and begin gathering facts, images, and possible artifacts.
Day 2Select 5 key artifacts to feature, write draft label cards, sketch layout of your display.
Day 3Create your exhibit board or model. Add visuals, titles, and neat labeling.
Day 4Write 1-page curator’s summary explaining your exhibit’s theme. Rehearse presentation.
Day 5Present exhibit to class. Submit reflection paragraph.

Option 2: Civil War Podcast or Interview Series

Record a historically accurate and creative 3–5 minute podcast or interview that brings a Civil War topic to life.

Suggested Materials

  • Laptop, tablet, or phone with microphone
  • Free audio recording app (Audacity, GarageBand, Voice Memos)
  • Script template or index cards
  • Background music or sound effects (optional)

5-Day Timeline

DayFocus & Tasks
Day 1Choose topic and role type. Research chosen event/person.
Day 2Write full script (intro, discussion, conclusion) including at least 3 historical references.
Day 3Record audio. Ensure clarity and pacing. Add effects or transitions if desired.
Day 4Edit recording for mistakes, add background sounds, finalize script draft.
Day 5Share podcast with class. Submit reflection paragraph.

Option 3: Civil War Soldier’s Diary

Write a creative yet historically grounded diary that tells the story of a soldier, nurse, or freed person during the war.

Suggested Materials

  • Notebook or digital document
  • Historical maps, timelines, or letters for inspiration
  • Colored pencils or pens for drawings
  • Optional: small journal booklet for final copy

5-Day Timeline

DayFocus & Tasks
Day 1Decide your character and research their background.
Day 2Identify 3–4 real events your character will mention. Draft first two diary entries.
Day 3Write remaining 3 entries including emotions and realistic experiences.
Day 4Add maps or sketches. Edit for clarity and historical accuracy.
Day 5Compile final diary. Submit reflection paragraph.

Option 4: Civil War Monument or Memorial Design

Design a monument or memorial that honors a person, group, or event from the Civil War and explains its symbolism.

Suggested Materials

  • Poster board, cardboard, or modeling clay
  • Sketching paper or digital design tool
  • Markers, rulers, paints, glue
  • Reference images of real monuments

5-Day Timeline

DayFocus & Tasks
Day 1Choose focus and research significance.
Day 2Draft 2–3 designs. Decide on materials, colors, symbols. Write notes explaining each part.
Day 3Build or draw final monument. Add title and labels.
Day 4Write plaque inscription or dedication speech. Finalize visual design.
Day 5Present design. Submit reflection paragraph.

Option 5: Civil War News Project

Design a historically accurate and visually engaging Civil War newspaper front page or news broadcast.

Suggested Materials

  • Poster board or Google Slides/Docs
  • Rulers, markers, colored pencils
  • Access to online archives for political cartoons or primary sources
  • Optional: microphone and camera if creating a broadcast version

5-Day Timeline

DayFocus & Tasks
Day 1Choose focus (battle, Lincoln speeches, homefront). Research key facts/events.
Day 2Draft 3 news stories including quotes, summaries, perspectives.
Day 3Arrange layout with headlines, columns, images, and political cartoon.
Day 4Add bylines, datelines, color. Edit spelling and historical accuracy.
Day 5Present front page or broadcast. Submit reflection paragraph.
